About Safety Audit

A safety audit checklist evaluates an organization’s overall health and safety performance through a thorough and unbiased assessment of its programs and procedures. The resulting safety audit report provides insights into how effective and dependable these safety measures are in maintaining a secure workplace environment.

Types of Safety Audits

A safety audit enables an organization to assess the performance of its safety programs. There are three main types of safety audits:

  • Compliance Audit – This type of audit examines whether the organization adheres to all applicable workplace safety laws and regulations.
  • Program Audit – This audit measures the overall effectiveness of the organization’s safety initiatives by reviewing how safety programs are designed and implemented in practice.
  • Management Audit – Combining elements of both compliance and program audits, this audit evaluates the organization’s safety policies and their alignment with Occupational Health and Safety Management System requirements. It also incorporates employee feedback to gain deeper insights into workplace safety practices.

The Safety Audit Checklist

A safety audit checklist serves to identify potential hazards and risk areas that may impact workplace safety. It provides a systematic approach to evaluating and improving safety standards within an organization. The checklist typically includes:

  • Assessing confined spaces, elevated areas, and other restricted zones.
  • Examining electrical systems, compressed air installations, and surfaces prone to slipping.
  • Identifying the presence of hazardous substances such as gases, asbestos, dust, and airborne contaminants.
  • Evaluating risks related to water exposure, vehicle movement, manual material handling, and overhead dangers.
  • Categorizing risk levels based on likelihood, severity, and overall risk rating.
  • Proposing appropriate control measures to mitigate common workplace hazards and risks.

Who Should Use a Safety Audit Checklist

A safety audit checklist is essential for organizations across all industries, regardless of their size, nature, or geographical location. It plays a crucial role in promoting workplace safety and ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory standards. The checklist is particularly relevant for a wide range of entities, including:

  • Vendors
  • Manufacturers
  • Retailers
  • Information Technology companies
  • Educational institutions

By systematically evaluating compliance with the Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OHSMS) and other workplace safety regulations, a safety audit checklist helps organizations minimize risks, enhance safety performance, and prevent potential fines or penalties arising from non-compliance.

Key Items to Include in Your Checklist

The primary objective of a safety audit is to identify potential hazards and risks within the workplace and recommend suitable control measures to maintain a safe and healthy environment. A comprehensive safety audit checklist should include the following aspects:

  • Work Processes – Evaluate all operational procedures to identify potential risks associated with specific tasks. Ensure that effective control measures are in place to protect employees and demonstrate the organization’s commitment to safety compliance.
  • Fire Safety and Emergency Preparedness – Incorporate preventive measures to minimize fire hazards and establish a well-defined evacuation plan to ensure the safety of all personnel during emergencies.
  • oading and Unloading Operations – For organizations involved in frequent material handling, assess and standardize procedures to prevent accidents and injuries during loading and unloading activities.
  • Lighting and Electrical Safety – Ensure that workplace lighting meets adequate standards to promote visibility, productivity, and safety. Regularly inspect electrical systems to prevent potential hazards.
  • Tools and Machinery Safety – Evaluate the use and maintenance of machinery, tools, and vehicles to confirm that appropriate safety measures are implemented and employees are properly trained in their operation.
