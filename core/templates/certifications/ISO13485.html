About ISO 13485 Certification

ISO 13485:2016 is an internationally recognized standard that defines the requirements for a Quality Management System (QMS) specific to the design and manufacture of medical devices.

ISO 13485:2016 is an internationally recognized standard that defines the requirements for a Quality Management System (QMS) specific to the design, development, and manufacture of medical devices. It provides a comprehensive framework to ensure that organizations consistently meet both customer expectations and applicable regulatory requirements in the medical device industry.

The standard applies to organizations involved in all stages of the medical device lifecycle — including design, development, production, storage, distribution, installation, and servicing. By adhering to ISO 13485, companies demonstrate their commitment to maintaining product safety, reliability, and quality while complying with international regulatory frameworks.

ISO 13485 establishes a structured approach to implementing an effective Medical Device Quality Management System (MD-QMS). It helps organizations identify and manage risks associated with equipment contamination, product handling errors, and other operational challenges. Through systematic risk assessment and preventive planning, the standard enhances process efficiency and minimizes potential failures.

Certification to ISO 13485:2016 serves as tangible evidence of an organization’s capability to deliver high-quality, safe, and compliant medical devices and related services. It reinforces customer confidence and facilitates access to global markets by aligning with recognized regulatory expectations.

ISO 13485 Certification Scope and Coverage

Non-Active Medical Devices (A.1)

Initially, the MD-QMS accreditation covered one Main Technical Area (MTA), focusing on non-active medical devices. This includes:

  • A.1.1 General Non-Active, Non-Implantable Medical Devices – Devices that operate without power, such as surgical instruments, bandages, and other basic medical tools.
  • A.1.2 Non-Active Implants – Includes medical implants like orthopedic screws, plates, and dental implants.
  • A.1.3 Devices for Wound Care – Products designed to support healing, such as dressings and wound-closure strips.
  • A.1.4 Non-Active Dental Devices and Accessories – Instruments and accessories used in dental procedures.
  • A.1.5 Other Non-Active Medical Devices – Additional non-powered devices that fall under this category.

In Vitro Diagnostic (IVD) Medical Devices (A.4)

Following the 2024–25 surveillance audit, the scope of MD-QMS was expanded to include three additional MTAs, including IVD medical devices. These include:

  • A.4.1 Reagents, Calibrators, and Control Materials for diagnostic testing in areas such as:
    • Clinical Chemistry
    • Immunochemistry (Immunology)
    • Hematology / Haemostasis / Immunohematology
    • Microbiology
    • Infectious Immunology
    • Histology / Cytology
    • Genetic Testing
  • A.4.2 In Vitro Diagnostic Instruments and Software – Devices and related software used in diagnostic testing.
  • A.4.3 Other IVD Medical Devices – Additional products classified under the IVD category.
  • Sterilization Methods for Medical Devices (A.5)

    • Ensuring medical devices are contamination-free is critical. ISO 13485 includes compliance with sterilization processes such as:

    • A.5.1 Ethylene Oxide Gas Sterilization (EOG) – Used for heat-sensitive devices.
    • A.5.2 Moist Heat – Common method using steam under pressure.
    • A.5.3 Aseptic Processing – Ensures sterile environments during production.
    • A.5.4 Radiation Sterilization – Uses gamma rays, e-beams, or X-rays.
    • A.5.5 Other Sterilization Methods – Covers additional approved sterilization techniques.
  • Distribution, Parts, and Services (A.7)

    • Medical device quality management extends beyond manufacturing. It covers supply chain aspects such as:

    • A.7.1 Raw Materials – Ensuring the quality of materials used in production.
    • A.7.2 Components – Verification of components for medical device assembly.
    • A.7.3 Subassemblies – Ensuring subassembled products meet quality standards.
    • A.7.4 Calibration Services – Regular calibration of medical devices and equipment.
    • A.7.5 Distribution Services – Ensuring safe transportation and handling.
    • A.7.6 Maintenance Services – Routine servicing of medical equipment.
    • A.7.7 Transportation Services – Compliance in transporting medical devices securely.

ISO 13485 Requirements

The Annex SL High-Level Structure of the ISO 22000 standard outlines the mandatory elements required for the successful establishment and implementation of a Food Safety Management System (FSMS) within an organization. These essential requirements are defined in Sections 4 through 10 of the Annex SL framework. The initial three sections primarily serve as an introduction, detailing the standard’s scope, normative references, and key terms and definitions. The subsequent seven sections, however, specify the core operational and management requirements necessary to ensure an effective and compliant FSMS.

Clearly identify the purpose, target market, and intended use of the medical device. Consider relevant regional and international regulations to ensure the scope is comprehensive and compliant.

Assess your existing Quality Management System (QMS) against ISO 13485 requirements. Identify any non-conformities or gaps that need to be addressed to achieve compliance.

Use insights from the gap analysis to create a corrective action plan. This plan should address all identified gaps and ensure alignment with ISO 13485 requirements as well as applicable regional regulations.

Prepare and organize all mandatory documentation, including the Quality Manual, procedures, work instructions, and evidence of implementation. Proper documentation ensures audit readiness and demonstrates compliance.

Educate employees involved in medical device production on ISO 13485 requirements, including legal, regulatory, and procedural obligations, to ensure proper understanding and adherence.

Execute the established Medical Device Quality Management System (MD-QMS) for a minimum period of three months, documenting all processes and activities to demonstrate operational effectiveness.

Perform internal audits to verify compliance with ISO 13485 requirements. Audits can be conducted by qualified internal personnel or an independent third party to identify and correct deviations.

Evaluate the effectiveness of the QMS through a management review, ensuring that all processes, procedures, and corrective actions align with ISO 13485 standards.

Engage an accredited certification body to conduct the formal audit of your QMS. Upon successful completion, your organization will be awarded ISO 13485 certification.
