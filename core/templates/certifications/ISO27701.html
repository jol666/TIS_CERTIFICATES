About ISO/IEC 27701:

ISO 27701 is an international standard that defines the framework for a Privacy Information Management System (PIMS), sometimes referred to as a Personal Information Management System. It provides guidance for organizations on how Personally Identifiable Information (PII) Controllers and PII Processors can effectively manage data privacy within their IT operations.

The standard outlines requirements for the establishment, implementation, maintenance, and continual improvement of a PIMS. It offers a structured approach for both data controllers and data processors to ensure the secure handling of personal information.

By adopting ISO 27701, organizations gain access to tools and methodologies to implement appropriate controls for safeguarding personal data. The standard emphasizes a risk-based approach, enabling organizations to identify potential privacy risks and select suitable measures to enhance both current and future operations.

Overview of ISO/IEC 27701

ISO/IEC 27701 is an internationally recognized standard designed to establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve a Privacy Information Management System (PIMS). It supports organizations in ensuring compliance with global privacy regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). The standard primarily focuses on managing and mitigating privacy risks related to the handling of Personally Identifiable Information (PII), promoting transparency, accountability, and data protection across all organizational processes.

Difference between ISO/IEC 27701:2019 and ISO/IEC 27701:2025

The primary distinction between ISO/IEC 27701:2019 Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) and ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Information Security, Cybersecurity, and Privacy Protection — Privacy Information Management Systems lies in their structural approach. The 2019 edition functions as an extension of ISO/IEC 27001 (Information Security Management System – ISMS), whereas the 2025 edition has evolved into a standalone standard. This transformation enables organizations to implement and certify a PIMS independently, without requiring prior ISO 27001 certification, thereby expanding accessibility and adoption for privacy-focused initiatives.

Key Highlights

  • Accessibility of Certification: The 2019 version limited certification to organizations already certified under ISO/IEC 27001, as it was built upon the ISMS framework. The 2025 edition removes this dependency, allowing organizations to pursue independent PIMS certification, regardless of their ISO 27001 status.
  • Enhanced Controls and Alignment: The 2025 update introduces approximately 31 new controls for PII controllers and 18 for PII processors, ensuring alignment with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and ISO/IEC 27002:2022. It retains 29 information security controls directly relevant to privacy while removing 52 non-privacy-related controls from the 2019 version, resulting in a more targeted and efficient framework.
  • Structural Enhancements: The revised standard strengthens its relevance to modern digital challenges such as artificial intelligence (AI), cloud computing, and cybersecurity. New clauses emphasize independent evaluation (Clause 9), resource management and awareness (Clause 7), and continuous improvement (Clause 10), reflecting a more dynamic and risk-based approach.
  • Transition and Implementation: The 2019 edition was officially withdrawn on October 14, 2025. Organizations currently certified under the 2019 framework should initiate transition plans in coordination with accreditation bodies, which are expected to release detailed transition guidelines.

Beyond regulatory compliance, ISO/IEC 27701:2025 serves as a strategic framework for privacy governance, enabling organizations to strengthen trust, enhance competitiveness, and demonstrate accountability in managing personally identifiable information (PII).

Difference between ISO 27701 Certification and ISO 27001 Certification

ISO/IEC 27701:2019 Certification is an extension of the ISO 27001 standard, designed to enhance data privacy management. Unlike ISO/IEC 27001:2013, which primarily focuses on managing information security risks and implementing security controls, ISO/IEC 27701:2019 emphasizes reducing data protection and information privacy risks. It provides a framework for organizations to handle personally identifiable information (PII) responsibly and serves as a reliable standard for compliance with regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

When ISO 27701 Certification Was Released

ISO 27701 Certification is an internationally recognized standard, published in August 2019, and marks the first global framework specifically focused on Privacy Information Management Systems (PIMS).

Building on an existing Information Security Management System (ISMS), this standard helps organizations implement, maintain, and continuously improve their PIMS. It is applicable across all industries, regardless of size, type, number of branches, or operational complexity.

ISO 27701 Requirements

The High-Level Structure (HLS) of ISO 27701 Certification is based on the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) methodology. Aligned with Annex SL, the standard comprises ten sections, with the first three serving as introductory guidance and the remaining seven providing auditable requirements for implementing a Privacy Information Management System (PIMS). The framework includes essential requirements to ensure the effective establishment, operation, and continual improvement of PIMS within an organization.

This section focuses on identifying all processes, operations, and activities that fall under the scope of ISO/IEC 27701 Certification, ensuring an effective Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) within the organization.

This section highlights the critical role of top management and auditors in implementing PIMS. It clearly defines leadership responsibilities to prevent conflicts and ensure proper governance of the privacy management system.

This section covers the establishment of objectives for the management system and the identification and analysis of risks, enabling organizations to proactively address and mitigate potential privacy threats.

This section outlines the necessary tools, technologies, and resources required for PIMS implementation. It also defines requirements related to competence, awareness, documentation control, and maintenance of information in accordance with the standard.

This section addresses operational processes and evaluates progress toward achieving the organization’s objectives. A key requirement is the regular assessment and management of risks.

This section emphasizes the periodic review of the management system to verify the effectiveness of arrangements, processes, and controls. Management must monitor processes, business activities, and operations to maintain an effective privacy management system.

This section ensures the ongoing effectiveness of the privacy management system. It focuses on continual improvement to address risks and enhance the overall management framework.

Importance of ISO 27701 Certification

The ISO 27701 standard is applicable to organizations of all sizes and industries, regardless of their location. It provides a structured framework for managing data privacy in alignment with an existing Information Security Management System (ISMS), enabling organizations to establish an effective Privacy Information Management System (PIMS).

Achieving ISO 27701 Certification helps organizations demonstrate compliance with privacy laws and regulations, reducing the risk of regulatory penalties. Additionally, it offers several key benefits:

  • Enhances trust and confidence – Strengthens users’ trust in the organization, supporting customer retention and attracting new clients.
  • Provides a competitive advantage – Positions the organization as a leader in privacy and data protection practices.
  • Builds resilience and agility – Establishes a robust privacy management framework capable of adapting to evolving challenges.
  • Ensures regulatory compliance – Integrates relevant laws and standards, including GDPR, to maintain consistent adherence to privacy and data protection requirements.
