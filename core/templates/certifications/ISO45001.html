About ISO 45001 Certification

ISO 45001, also known as the Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OHSMS), provides a comprehensive framework to ensure the safety and well-being of employees. It is an international standard focused on process improvement, adopting a process-centered approach to quality management that spans the entire enterprise, including the customer experience. The standard equips organizations with a set of regulatory norms, enabling them to operate proactively rather than reactively. ISO 45001 also serves as a business localization tool, allowing organizations to understand how overall quality management supports marketing initiatives and facilitates supply chain management.

The standard addresses four key dimensions: Process, Product, People, and Organization. ISO 45001 outlines the essential requirements for establishing a quality management system, emphasizing generic principles that help organizations optimize core processes to meet customer needs effectively. Additionally, it highlights four interrelated focus areas: People, Environment and Performance, Information and Communication, and Leadership.

ISO 45001: Occupational Health & Safety Management System

ISO 45001:2018 Certification establishes the framework for implementing an Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OHSMS) within organizations. Published in March 2018, this standard focuses on preventing work-related injuries, illnesses, and hazards. It follows the common High-Level Structure (HLS) shared with ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 14001:2015, facilitating seamless integration with existing management systems.

As the successor to OHSAS 18001, ISO 45001 enables straightforward migration for organizations already certified under the previous standard. While the original three-year migration window received extensions due to the COVID-19 pandemic, organizations should note that ISO 45001 introduces significant enhancements beyond its predecessor, despite their common foundation.

ISO 45001:2018 – Successor to OHSAS 18001 Certification

ISO 45001:2018 Certification is the updated replacement for the earlier OHSAS 18001 standard. The revised standard simplifies the integration of the Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OHSMS) with other management systems, as it follows the new common structure, Annex SL, used in ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 14001:2015.

Organizations seeking more information on ISO 45001:2018 and its differences from OHSAS 18001 can refer to detailed resources. For organizations already certified under OHSAS 18001, the original migration deadline to ISO 45001:2018 was 31st January.

However, due to the Covid-19 pandemic, this deadline has been extended. TIS Certifications has supported numerous organizations with online migration audits, as well as new ISO 45001 certification applications.

Evolution of ISO 45001 Compliance

ISO 45001 is a globally recognized standard from the ISO 45000 family for occupational health and safety management. First introduced in 1989 and revised in 2018, it replaced OHSAS 18001, which was officially withdrawn by BSI in 2021. The current version is ISO 45001:2018.

Getting Certified to ISO 45001 Certification

Once your Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OHSMS) is developed in accordance with ISO 45001, the certification process is straightforward. While the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) publishes the standards, certification is conducted by an accredited external ISO certification body. The basic steps for obtaining ISO 45001 certification are as follows:

  1. Develop and implement an ISO 45001-compliant management system.
  2. Schedule an audit with an accredited certification body.
  3. Audit review: An auditor evaluates your processes to ensure compliance with ISO 45001 requirements.
  4. Address gaps: Resolve any non-conformities identified during the audit.
  5. Certification: Once all requirements are met, the organization is formally certified to ISO 45001.

This structured approach ensures that your organization maintains compliance and demonstrates its commitment to occupational health and safety.

ISO 45001 Certification Requirements

The ISO 45001 Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OH&SMS) Certification outlines the essential requirements for establishing an effective management system. These requirements are organized into ten sections (Sections 1 to 10) and are structured around the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) framework to ensure continuous improvement and operational efficiency.

The ISO 45001 certification requirements checklist, structured around the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle, is outlined as follows:

  • Sections 1 to 6 correspond to the Planning phase.
  • Sections 1 to 3 serve as the introductory sections.
  • Sections 7 and 8 relate to the Implementation (Do) phase.
  • Section 9 pertains to the Evaluation (Check) phase.
  • Section 10 represents the Improvement (Act) phase.

The organization must identify all internal and external factors that may influence its operations. This helps define the scope of the Occupational Health and Safety (OH&S) policy and supports the establishment of an effective OH&S management framework.

The organization must allocate the necessary resources, including human, financial, and material, to effectively maintain the management system. It should ensure employees are competent through adequate training, education, and experience to fulfill their roles efficiently.

This involves defining and documenting operational processes and implementing control measures to manage and eliminate workplace hazards. It ensures that all occupational health and safety risks are properly addressed through structured procedures and policies.

ISO 45001 requires organizations to monitor, measure, and analyze their processes to identify areas of nonconformity. It also assesses overall OH&S performance to determine opportunities for improvement and ensure compliance with set objectives.
